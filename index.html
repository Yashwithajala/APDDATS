Common Diseases

Die Back

High Severity Mango

Twigs begin to die from the top downwards, with leaves turning brown and remaining attached to the plant.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Prune affected branches 6-8 inches below infection
  • Apply copper oxychloride paste on cut ends Spray with carbendazim(0.1%)

Healthy Tree

No Disease Mango

A healthy mango tree has vibrant green leaves, strong branches, and shows normal growth patterns without any signs of disease.

Prevention Tips:

  • Regular inspection of trees
  • Proper irrigation and fertilization
  • Maintain good orchard hygiene

Bacterial Canker

High Severity Mango, Stone Fruits

Dark, water-soaked lesions on stems and branches that ooze bacterial exudate, leading to dieback of branches.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Prune infected branches below canker
  • Apply copper-based bactericides

Powdery Mildew

Medium Severity Mango, Grapes

White powdery fungal growth on leaves, flowers and young fruits, causing distortion and premature dropping.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Spray wettable sulfur (0.2%)
  • Apply potassium bicarbonate
  • Use resistant varieties

Gall Midge

Medium Severity Mango

Small maggots feed on tender leaves causing swollen, twisted galls that reduce photosynthetic area.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Remove and destroy affected leaves
  • Spray dimethoate (0.05%)
  • Use yellow sticky traps

leaf-cutting weevil

High Severity Mango

Beetles notch leaf margins and grubs bore into stems causing wilting and dieback of shoots.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Collect and destroy adults at night
  • Spray carbaryl (0.1%)
  • Remove and burn infested shoots

Anthracnose

High Severity Mango, Many Fruits

Dark, sunken lesions on fruits and black spots on leaves that spread rapidly in wet conditions.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Spray copper oxychloride (0.3%)
  • Apply mancozeb (0.2%)
  • Prune for better air circulation

Sooty Mould

Medium Severity Mango, Citrus

Black fungal growth on leaves and stems that develops on honeydew secreted by sap-sucking insects.

Recommended Treatment:

  • Control sap-sucking insects
  • Spray starch solution (1%)
  • Wipe leaves with mild soap solution
